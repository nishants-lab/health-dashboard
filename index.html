🔴 HDL — 35 → target >45

Needs: Exercise + Diet + Time
  • Aerobic exercise 150+ min/week — single biggest HDL raiser (5–10% increase per meta-analyses)
  • Omega-3: 2 tbsp ground flaxseed/day + walnuts + algae-based DHA capsule (veg alternative to fish oil)
  • Monounsaturated fats: 1–2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil daily, avocado, almonds
  • Eliminate trans fats completely (fried snacks, bakery items with vanaspati)
  • Coconut oil in moderation — it can raise HDL but also raises LDL
  • Lose visceral fat — even 3–5 kg loss improves HDL
HDL is the hardest lipid to raise through diet alone. Sustained aerobic exercise is the #1 lever. Thyroid improvement will add another 5–15% HDL boost on top.

  • Almonds — a handful (~15) daily. Proven 5–7% LDL reduction (Sabate et al., JAMA 2003). Normal snacking amount.
  • Soy protein 25g/day (tofu, soy milk, edamame)
  • Thyroid improvement will reduce LDL by 10–20% on its own
Your LDL is borderline — achievable with diet alone if thyroid is fixed. No statin needed at current levels unless other risk factors compound.

Why this split: Research shows 150+ min/week of moderate aerobic exercise raises HDL by 5–10% (meta-analysis: Kodama et al., Archives of Internal Medicine 2007). Zone 2 cardio (where you can hold a conversation but feel effort) is the most effective intensity for HDL improvement. Strength training improves insulin sensitivity, lowers LDL, and reduces visceral fat — but has less direct HDL effect than cardio.
Non-negotiable minimum: If you can't do the full plan, prioritize 3 days of 45-min Zone 2 cardio (Mon/Wed/Sat). This alone will move HDL more than any diet change.
Progression: Start at your current fitness. Week 1–4: build consistency. Week 5–8: increase duration/intensity by 10%. Retest lipids after 12 weeks.
